The Evolution of Basketball

Ever since 1891, basketball has continued to evolve, with new strategies being developed every era by the best teams. With the introduction of the three-point shot in 1979, one way the game has evolved is the increase in volume of three-point shots compared to the traditional two-pointer. But when did this occur? The transition towards a three-point era has been a rather recent development, with players like Stephen Curry and James Harden being pivotal influencers in this time. About Basketall: The Basics

Basketball is played with 10 players on the court at a time, 5 on each team. The objective of the game is to shoot the ball into a hoop at the end of the court, with the team with the most points at the end of the game being the winner. Teams advance the ball up the court either through dribbling (bouncing) or passing the ball. A team composition is usually made of 1 center, 2 forwards and 2 guards.

When a basket is made, the amount of points awarded is dependant on where the shot is made from. Shots are worth three-points if the shot is made behind the three-point line, two-points if the shot is made within the three-point arc and one-point if made during a free throw (refer to court on the right for reference).

Each game is divided into 4 quarters, with a larger break happening after the first half of the game. During game time, a shot clock is used in order to maintain gameflow. Teams have 24 seconds to score the ball, with the time pausing during stoppages of play or reset if the other team gains possession.

Fouls and violations are present in the game to ensure a baseline of rules. Some fouls include personal fouls, charging, blocking, flagrant foul and technical foul. Some violations include travelling, double dribble and goaltending.
