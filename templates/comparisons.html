Comparing 2008-09 And 2018-19 Seasons: Players And League

In order to see how basketball has changed over the years, it is important to compare where players shot from during different seasons. Below are visualsations of where players shot from during the 2008-09 season compared to the 2018-19 season as well as a summmary a comparison of shot selection league wide. Data used is sourced from the NBA API.

(Players available for comparison are only those who have played in both seasons).

Player Shot Comparison

It is evident that players have transitioned towards shooting more three-point shots than two-point shots, with some players with a noteworthry transition being Brook Lopez, Al Horford, Kevin Love, Marc Gasol and Trevor Ariza. In the case of Brook Lopez, during the 2008-09 season, he shot almost all of his shots from within the three-point line, with only two three-pointers attempted throughout the entire season. Compare this to his 2018-19 season where over 500 attempts were made from behind the three-point line.


Many of the players who have transitioned to more three-point attempts than two-point attempts play the center position in a team. Of the 5 players noted above, 4 play/have played as a center in their respective teams. With how the game has evolved, centers who used to only play inside the three-point arc are now stretching the floor and shooting a higher volume of three-point shots.

League Shot Comparison

Comparing the entire league between the 2008-09 and 2018-19 season shows that the number of shots in each zone remains somewhat consistent for the restricted area, in the paint and corner three-point shots, however there is a stark contrast between the number of shots attempted in the mid-range and above the break three between the seasons. The number of mid-range shot attempts has halved from 60 000+ attempts in 2008-09 to around 30 000 attempts in 2018-19. These shot attempts have transitioned to above the break three point shots, with attempts doubling from 30 000 in 2008-09 to 60 000+ in the 2018-19 season.


Leaguewide, field goal percentage largely remains similar when comparing percentages from both seasons, with only slight differences in the proportion of shots made in each shooting zone. Although shooting percentage drops from 2008-09 to 2018-19 when shooting from above the break three, a 1% drop in percentage is neglible when it coincides with double the amount of shots from three-points in 2018-19.



Summary

Teams are shooting more three-point shots due to a simple fact, the team with more points at the end of the game wins.

  • Two-point shots are worth 1 point per shot when shooting at 50%
  • Three-point shots are worth 1 point per shot when shooting at 33%
However, with a league average of approximately 37% from three-points, each three point shot is worth ~1.11 points. For a two-point shot to be worth 1.11 points, players would have to shoot 55.5% from two-points in order to match the three-point efficiency. The graphs show that this 55.5% is not reached in any two-point shot, leading to an increase in volume of three-point shots.
